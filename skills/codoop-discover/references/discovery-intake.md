# Discovery Intake Question Guide

Use this guide in rounds before formal Discovery. Ask no more than 2–3 questions per round and do not repeat information already known. Offer 2–3 plain-language options or short examples for each question, and always allow: “I’m not sure—please recommend.”

## Round 1: Product Idea and Goal (Required)

- What kind of app do you want to build? Describe it in one simple sentence.
- Who is it mainly for, and when do they run into this problem?
- What frustration should it remove, or what result should it help them achieve?
- If version one could do only one thing well, what must that be?
- What would make this successful first: people using it, time saved, proving the idea, revenue, or something else?

## Round 2: Core Flow and Scope (Required)

- What is the first thing you want a user to do after opening the app?
- What result or value should they get when they finish?
- What will users need to enter, upload, choose, create, or view?
- What features should explicitly stay out of version one?
- When a user returns after leaving, what should they be able to find or continue quickly?

## Round 3: Visual Direction (Required)

- What should a user feel at first glance: professional and trustworthy, friendly and light, efficient and tool-like, warm and playful, bold and distinctive, or something else?
- Are there products, websites, or apps you like or dislike? Ask what specifically they like or dislike, not only for names.
- Should the interface feel spacious and simple, or show more information at once?
- Do you already have a product name, logo, colors, fonts, images, icons, or other brand assets? Put existing assets in the project-root `resources/` directory and index them in `resources/README.md`.
- Are there visual constraints such as light/dark preference, night use, larger type, high contrast, or industry rules?

## Round 4: Product Conditions to Ask Only When Relevant

Ask these only when earlier answers make them relevant.

### Product Form and Devices

- Should this be a web app, mobile app, desktop app, or multiple platforms? Recommend based on the user’s situation when they are unsure.
- Does it rely on device features such as camera, location, microphone, notifications, offline use, or file uploads?
- Does it need sign-in? What can someone do without an account?

### User Types and Collaboration

- Are there multiple user types, such as customers, creators, merchants, or administrators?
- Does it need invites, sharing, comments, approval, collaboration, or an admin area?

### Business and Operations

- Will it use payment, subscriptions, advertising, transaction fees, or business purchasing? It is fine not to decide this yet.
- Does it need orders, membership, refunds, offers, or operational configuration?

### Data, Privacy, and Risk

- What data will it keep? Does it include photos, audio, location, health, financial, or other sensitive information?
- Do users need to export, delete, back up, or share their data?
- What failures must never happen, such as data loss, privacy leaks, or incorrect charges?

### Delivery Constraints and Existing Materials

- What already exists: idea notes, prototypes, code, a domain, brand assets, data, or third-party accounts?
- Are there deadlines, budget, team-size, language, region, or device constraints?
- Is version one for a demo, a small pilot, or a public launch?

## Finish: Confirm the Discovery Brief

Before role collaboration begins, summarize and ask the user to confirm:

- Product positioning and target users
- Main use case and core flow
- Version-one must-haves and explicit exclusions
- Visual direction, references, and existing brand assets
- Platform, business, data, and delivery constraints
- Open questions and a recommended assumption for each

Do not write a recommended assumption into later specifications as a locked requirement until the user confirms it.
